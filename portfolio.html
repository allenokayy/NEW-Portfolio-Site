portfolio.

I recently have been interested in beekeeping videos on YouTube and been thinking about what I would do if I were to keep bees of my own. I'm not sure if I'm going to keep bees anytime soon, but I do know I like to design. So I got to work on a logo design, anticipating that it would lead the design of all other elements. After many iterations, I finally landed on something I thought looked good enough to print out.

And it looked terrible. Uh oh! After some more tests, I realized the problem was I was overthinking it. Product design isn't just a logo - it's the whole package! And sometimes you need to know when to let the package do the talking, and hold back on the other aspects.

My client for this project was the small indie Youtuber Nyaoming, who was looking for social media ads to bring attention to the fact she was going to reopen her online shop. The ads were intended for her Instagram and Bluesky, so I went with a 1:1 aspect ratio to look good on both websites. The ads also needed to act as a catalogue for her new merchandise, so I made sure to have a consistent background to give a sense of cohesion even if the layout had to be dramatically changed to properly fit the content. She also requested a business card for her in person sales and to include with her packages. The colors were adjusted from the ads based on the client's wishes.

This was a school project where I was tasked to choose an annual reoccuring conference and create a booklet based upon their branding. I decided to challenge myself and chose TAAFI, a.k.a. Toronto Animation Arts Festival International. The reason this was a challenge was because TAAFI changes their branding yearly! I couldn't just rely on preexisting assets - I had to make my own based upon previous years. I believe I managed to create something very appealing and true to TAAFI within limited time constraints and could be applied to many different deliverables needed for a conference.
